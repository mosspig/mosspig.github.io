Reading / Listening Summaries

CounterSpin, and the The Dig, are providing a bit of an education on progressive viewpoints. The Dig, in particular, tends to interview academics and researchers with a Marxist bent who have recently published books. In some of the interviews on The Dig, one receives what feels like a graduate seminar discussion and mini-lecture alongside an explication of the contents of the text, with references made to current events. CounterSpin is intended more like a progressive, media studies-ish NPR. One project I'd like to begin below is to summarize the contents and for-me-salient points in several of the most compelling Dig episodes I've listened to. The ones that come to mind are: one on real estate and capital; another on the 'black atlantic'; and the one I'm listening to now on housing.
